The main problem that our supervisor shared to us is the lack of staff in the company, especially in the accounting department. And since most of the staff are homebased there are only 2-3 people left in the office.

From my point of view, I also think they lack some staff. I have noticed that the developers in the company take on multiple projects at the same time. And they also have to maintain the system by themselves and abide to the client's requests for sudden changes.

I think that is why sir Skip distributed the systems of the projects he was handling to us OJTs since he was the on in charge of 4 of them. And he hoped that we could help him trace the code to find some bugs and improve the system.

I would recommend they hire some staff for the company. Although they have recenly hired someone for their web development projects, they still need some staff for their accounting department.








During my time as a trainee in ResponsivCode, I've experienced being a team lead on a project, and how it's very important to always give updates and relay the concerns of the team to the superiors. I also got a chance to learn from my superiors some new technical skills, especially in web development.

I was also lucky enough to experience dealing with a client, and that taught me how different the client sees the product compared to the developers. Especially when dealing with a client that has little to no experience with advanced technology. And that it is vital that both parties meet at the same end, and come to a compromise.

I just wish that I could spend more time as a trainee in the company since I believe it is vital that a student should experience what it is like to develop a project that a company can and would use in the near future. And I think having more hours as an OJT can help teach students what to expect in the industry, and how to handle and manage themselves when they become professionals.

And later on, the client will choose which template he prefers the most.

NOTE: The project was reassigned to another team, and we were reassigned to the android project.


Android Project

The project was to create a service marketplace app that would provide individuals in need, with a faster, more dependable way to hire skilled and trustworthy service providers, whether it be for home services, beauty, auto mechanics, computer, arts, educations, and others.

My task was to create the profile page, and the home page of the app.

NOTE: The project was momentarily discontinued due to problems with the equipment.

Inventory System

Sir Skip assigned each of us OJTs to a certain system. Mine was the inventory system. He instructed us to study how the code was formed, so that if the client requests changes, or if bugs appear, then we will be able to handle it on our own. But since we don't have much experience on web development, he gave us some time to do some self-study and practice on Bootstrap, JavaScript, CodeIgniter, MySQL, PHP, AJAX, and JQuery.

Inventory Sales System

Sir Skip assigned me to a new system, the inventory Sales. I was first tasked to create the database structure using MySQL. So I referred to the previous inventory system that was assigned to me. And since the PHP MyAdmin can not support numerous tables and lags all the time, I used the software SQLyog to create the tables needed for the database.
